Measures

Measures have been around for many years in the enterprise versions of Microsoft BI tools, such as SQL Server Analysis Services. Measures have now made it into the world of business users who want to create Power BI reports.

What is a measure?

A measure is simply a DAX formula that instructs Power BI to do a calculation on data. In a sense, a measure is a lot like a formula in a cell in Excel. The main difference between a formula in a cell in Excel and a meaure is that a measure always operates over the entire data model, not just over a few cells in a spreadheet. You can think of a measuer as a formula that calcualtes a result from the loaded data

the formula bar

With Power BI you write measures in the formula bar as shown below. The formula bar is not visisble unless you have a field selected in the Fields pane on the rigfht-hand side of Power BI. When you select a measure in the Fields pane, the formula bar appears just below the ribbon
